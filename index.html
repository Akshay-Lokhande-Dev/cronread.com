How to Write Cron Expressions

A standard cron expression has 5 fields separated by spaces: minute hour day-of-month month day-of-week

  • Minute — 0–59
  • Hour — 0–23 (UTC on most servers)
  • Day of month — 1–31
  • Month — 1–12 (or JAN–DEC)
  • Day of week — 0–6 (0 = Sunday, or SUN–SAT)

Special characters: * (any) · */n (every n) · a-b (range) · a,b (list)

AWS EventBridge Cron Format

AWS uses a 6-field format: minute hour day-of-month month day-of-week year. The day-of-month and day-of-week fields cannot both be set — one must be ?. This tool handles that conversion automatically.

Common Examples

  • 0 12 * * * — Every day at noon UTC
  • 0 9 * * 1-5 — Every weekday at 9 AM UTC
  • */5 * * * * — Every 5 minutes
  • 0 0 1 * * — First day of every month at midnight
  • 0 0 * * 0 — Every Sunday at midnight (same as @weekly)

Advanced Cron Syntax: L, W, and #

Beyond the standard five fields, many cron schedulers support extended special characters for more precise scheduling:

  • L (Last) — Refers to the last day of the month or week. For example, L in the day-of-month field means the last day of the month. 5L means the last Friday of the month.
  • W (Nearest Weekday) — Finds the nearest weekday (Mon–Fri) to a given day. 15W triggers on the weekday closest to the 15th of the month — avoiding weekends automatically.
  • # (Nth Weekday) — Specifies the Nth occurrence of a weekday in a month. 1#2 means the second Monday of the month. Useful for schedules like "every second Tuesday."

Frequently Asked Questions

What does */5 mean in a cron expression?
*/5 means "every 5 units". In the minutes field, */5 runs at 0, 5, 10, 15, 20... In the hours field, */5 runs at 0:00, 5:00, 10:00, 15:00, 20:00.
What is the AWS EventBridge cron format?
AWS EventBridge uses 6 fields: minute hour day-of-month month day-of-week year. You cannot set both day-of-month and day-of-week — one must be ?. Example: cron(0 12 * * ? *) runs every day at noon UTC.
What does @weekly mean in cron?
@weekly equals 0 0 * * 0 — once a week at midnight UTC on Sunday.
How do I run a cron job every weekday at 9 AM?
Use 0 9 * * 1-5. The 1-5 in the day-of-week field means Monday through Friday. You can also type "every weekday at 9am" in CronRead Pro to generate this automatically.
What does */15 9-17 * * * mean?
*/15 9-17 * * * means every 15 minutes between 9 AM and 5 PM UTC, every day. The */15 in the minute field fires at :00, :15, :30, :45. The 9-17 in the hour field restricts it to business hours.
How do I run a cron job on the first day of every month?
Use 0 0 1 * * — this runs at midnight UTC on the 1st of every month. This is equivalent to the @monthly macro.
Does GitHub Actions use UTC for cron schedules?
Yes. GitHub Actions cron schedules always run in UTC. There is no timezone field in the workflow — you must convert your local time to UTC. CronRead Pro's timezone selector helps you see exactly what UTC time corresponds to your local schedule.
What is the difference between cron and rate expressions in AWS?
AWS EventBridge supports both cron() and rate() expressions. rate(5 minutes) runs every 5 minutes on a fixed interval from when the rule was created. cron() gives precise control over exactly when something runs using a calendar-based schedule.
